Paricutin Volcano

  • In 1943 the Partcutin Volcano erupted in the state of Michoaco, Mexico.

  • The first man to witness the eruption was a Tarascan Indian farmer, named Dominic Pulido.

  • The Partcutinn is a Monogenetic cone, which means it stems from a single point of eruption.

  • The volcano now stands at 410 metres above ground.

  • Its hardened lava covers 16 square kilometres and its volcanic sand covers 32 square kilometres.

  • Named after a small Tarascan Indian village, particutin is now counted as one of the seven wonders of the world.
